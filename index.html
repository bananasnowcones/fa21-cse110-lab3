Summary: What's the whole point of this?

Professor Powell for the past few lectures, have been challenging us to think about software engineering differently. It is NOT simply coding or putting yourself in a fixed frame, but always constantly adapting to needs and systems, keeping in mind many factors such as productive habits, tools, constaints, etc. Of course, software engineering is much more than just coding itself; there's also mental and physical aspects to keep in mind like learning how to communicate and work in a team, sleep, stress, productivity, thinking habits, time management, and so on and so forth. It is a challenging concept to think about - a software engineer isn't about just the programming.
